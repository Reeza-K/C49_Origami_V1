Fun Facts about Camles:

  • Camal humps store fat. Not water like many believe.
  • Camels have three sets of eyelids and two rows of eyelashes to keep sand out of their eyes.
  • Camels have thick lips which let them forage for thorny plants other animals can't eat.

Fun Facts about Chameleons:

  • Chameleons have camoflauge making them sometimes seem invisible. They also change color based on their moods.
  • Chameleons have eyes in the backs of their heads.
  • Chameleons have extremely powerful tongues.

Fun Facts about Pigeons:

  • Pigeons and Doves are of the same species.
  • Pigeons are incredibly complex and intelligent animals.
  • Pigeons are renowned for their outstanding navigational abilities.
  • They use a range of skills, such as using the sun as a guide and an internal ‘magnetic compass’

Fun Facts about Teddy Bears:

  • The first Teddy-bear was created in 1902.
  • The Teddy Bears' Picnic song was originally called The Teddy Bear Two Step.
  • The Oxford English Dictionary dates the first use of the term teddy bear to 1906.

Fun Facts about Pandas:

  • Panda's diets maily consist of banbo even though it contains very little nutrition.
  • An adult can eat 12–38 kilos of bamboo per day.
  • Pandas go from pink to white and black (or brown).

Fun Facts about Cicadas:

  • Hundreds of cicadas emerge about every 13-17 years.
  • Cicadas Live on All Continents Except Antarctica.
  • They Have One of the Longest Insect Life Spans.
